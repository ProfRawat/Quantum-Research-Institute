Welcome to the IBM-HBCU Quantum Center. Our mission is to build a sustainable, diverse pipeline of quantum talent by empowering HBCU students and faculty to lead in Quantum Information Science and Engineering.

Read Full Message

Through coordinated access to IBM Quantum hardware, faculty development, student research programs, and national collaborations, the Center has created one of the largest quantum education and research initiatives across HBCUs.

As we look beyond 2026, our focus is on sustaining this ecosystem, expanding research capacity, and ensuring that Black students and institutions remain central to the quantum future.

About the Center

History & Establishment

The IBM-HBCU Quantum Center was established in 2020 as a partnership between IBM and a consortium of HBCUs, with Howard University serving as the lead institution.

The Center was formally announced at the IBM Quantum Network Summit and designed as a multiyear investment to prepare and develop Black talent from all STEM disciplines for the quantum future.

Vision & Mission

  • Increase the number of Black students educated in QISE (quantum systems and technologies).
  • Strengthen QISE research efforts at HBCUs.
  • Provide scholarships, fellowships, and internships.
  • Empower HBCUs to lead in the quantum workforce.

Strategic Pillars

  • Build: Foster community and belonging in quantum spaces.
  • Support: Enable research through funding, mentorship, and access.
  • Connect: Link talent to researchers, institutions, and industry.

Member Institutions & Regions

The Center encompasses 27 HBCU member institutions organized into five geographic regions (Mid-Atlantic, Virginia, Carolinas, Southeastern, Southwestern), each with designated research focus areas in quantum computing, hardware, education, and fundamental science.

Center PI: Dr. Danda B. Rawat

Dr. Danda B. Rawat

Principal Investigator

Biography: Dr. Danda B. Rawat is Associate Dean for Research & Graduate Studies and Full Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering & Computer Science at Howard University. He is the Founding Director of the Howard University Data Science & Cybersecurity Center, Founding Director of the DoD Center of Excellence in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning (CoE‑AIML), Director of the Quantum Research (QResearch) Lab, TruAI Research Lab, and Cyber-security and Wireless Networking Innovations (CWiNs) Research Lab, and Director of the Graduate Cybersecurity Certificate Program. He also served as PI and Founding Executive Director of the Research Institute for Tactical Autonomy (RITA), the 15th University Affiliated Research Center (UARC) of the U.S. Department of Defense. His research focuses on cybersecurity, artificial intelligence and machine learning, big data analytics, wireless networking, cyber‑physical systems, IoT, smart cities, software‑defined systems, and vehicular networks. Dr. Rawat has secured more than $110 million in research funding as PI and over $18 million as Co‑PI from federal agencies, industry, and foundations. He is a recipient of numerous honors including the NSF CAREER Award, DHS Scientific Leadership Award, Howard University President’s Medal of Achievement, Provost’s Distinguished Service Award, and multiple best paper awards. He has published more than 400 scholarly articles and 11 books, supervised 45 PhD graduates, mentored over 120 minority undergraduate students, and serves in editorial and leadership roles across major IEEE, ACM, and international research communities. Dr. Rawat earned his Ph.D. from Old Dominion University and is a Fellow of AAAS and IET, an ACM Distinguished Speaker, and an IEEE Distinguished Lecturer.

Research Interests: Quantum networking, quantum computing, cyber-physical systems, AI/ML, networking, cybersecurity, and secure distributed systems.

Leadership Roles: Center PI of the IBM-HBCU Quantum Center and has successfully led multi-institutional research and workforce initiatives such as CoE-AIML, RITA UARC, Data Science & Cybersecurity Center, etc..

Major Achievements: Received $110 million as PI and $20 million as Co-PI from federal agencies and leading tech industrices.
